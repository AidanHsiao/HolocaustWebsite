The legacy of Carl Spaatz
Carl Spaatz was an American general who served during World War II, and his war efforts made him stand out among his peers. Along with Omar Bradley, Spaatz was considered by general Dwight D. Eisenhower to be one of the most influential general during the war against Germany. Spaatz is well known for being the first chief of staff of the Independent Air Force, and helped lead the air power during the war. Spaatz was also responsible for many strategic bombings in enemy territories, where he chose specific locations and times to severely set back axis forces. After retiring from the Air Force, Spaatz died on July 14, 1974, at the age of 83.
